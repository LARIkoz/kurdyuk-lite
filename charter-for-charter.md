# Charter for Charter

How to write product charters. Four principles.

1. **No heuristics.** No numbers, no percentages, no thresholds. Principle works in 5 years when the market has changed. Data lives in the research artifacts. Principles live in the charter.

2. **No HOW.** WHAT matters, not HOW to do it. "Human language everywhere" — principle. "Use GPT-4 for translation" — implementation.

3. **Every principle = a test.** Point at code and say "violates P3." If a principle can't be violated — it's useless.

4. **Write, then cut.** If a principle needs more than 3 sentences — it's hiding multiple principles or slipping into implementation. Strip to the core. What's left after you remove everything removable — that's the principle.

## Anti-pattern: overloading

Before:

> Revenue is the top priority. Paywall is expected and normal. Do not optimize for free at the expense of revenue. Dark patterns and deception are not revenue — they are debt. Purchases survive updates and device changes. Free features stay free — no retroactive paywalling.

Six sentences. Four ideas. Two contradict ("free stays free" vs "revenue top priority").

After:

> **The app exists to make money.** Persuasion is business. Deception is debt.

Three sentences. One boundary. Everything else is implementation.
